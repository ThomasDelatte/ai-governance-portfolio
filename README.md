# Practical AI Governance Portfolio

A public portfolio focused on **AI Governance**, **Responsible AI**, **GenAI governance**, **EU AI Act implementation**, and **model risk management**.

This repository shows how abstract governance frameworks can be translated into concrete, reviewable artefacts: model cards, risk assessments, fairness assessments, monitoring plans, EU AI Act classification templates, governance operating models, and executive briefings.

The portfolio reflects a hybrid profile combining:

- Data science experience in banking
- Legal and EU policy background
- Experience in regulated environments
- Interest in practical AI governance, Responsible AI, model risk, and AI policy implementation

The objective is not to present production systems or client work. All projects are based on **public datasets, simulated scenarios, and hypothetical governance assumptions**, clearly identified in each project.

## How to Use This Repository

Each project folder contains:

- `README.md` — scenario context, objectives, assumptions, governance scope, and artefact map
- `artefacts/` — governance documents written as practical review artefacts
- `notebooks/` — technical analysis where relevant, mainly for the credit scoring project

Each project can be read independently as a standalone governance case study.

The projects are designed to be standalone, but they also build a coherent portfolio narrative:

1. **Project 01** shows governance of a traditional ML model.
2. **Project 02** extends the governance approach to GenAI and RAG systems.
3. **Project 03** moves from individual systems to enterprise-level AI governance readiness.

## Frameworks and References Covered

The portfolio draws on practical concepts from:

- EU AI Act
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- ISO/IEC 42001
- Model risk management practices
- Responsible AI governance principles
- Data governance and documentation practices
- GenAI risk management patterns
- Financial-services governance and control environments

The repository does not aim to reproduce these frameworks in full. Instead, it shows how they can be operationalised through concrete documents and controls.
